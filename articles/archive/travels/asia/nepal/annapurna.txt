27.2<br>
Trekki lahti kayntiin Pokharan guesthouselta. Reppu jai taakse, varustuksena kaksi olkakassia joiden sisalto lyhykaisyydessaan: vaihtosukat, vaihtokalsarit, vaihtopaita, makuupussi, Nikolta saadut fliissipaita ja ohut takki, hitonmoinen kasa rahaa ja perus hygieeniatarvikkeet (eli hammasharja ja saippua ja ripulilaakkeet). Matka bussiasemalle oli semipitka, ja pienen hakemisen jalkeen se loytyi, ehka tunnin kavely nihkeassa liikenteessa. Pienen hakemisen jalkeen loytyi myos 'onkalo' jossa myytiin bussilippuja ja nappasin bussin kohti Nayapulia. Bussi lahos tietenkin ekan 10 kilometrin jalkeen, jonka jalkeen oli vaihto jo valmiiksi tayteen bussiin, eli paasin/jouduin katolle. Tasta seurasikin ehka kuumottavin bussireissu ikina, kun bussi kiipesi 500m korkealle harjulle ja ylhaalla eras poika taputti meikalaista selkaan ja sano jotain tyyliin "pida kiinni". Bussi latkas ylhaalla vaihteen vapaalle ja sitten mentiin. Istuin viela reunalla, jalat ulkopuolella ja pidin kasilla kiinni tangosta jonka paalla myos istuin. Jalat tuppas lipsumaan bussin reunan yli jokaisella toyssylla ja ulkomutkissa pysty kurkisteleen alapuolella nakyviin rotkomaisemiin kun bussi 'hieman' kallistui. Vauhtia oli ainakin 60km/h liikaa tilannenopeuteen verrattuna.<br>
Kaikin puolin mahtavin vuoristoratakokemus, onneksi loytyy puristusvoimaa sormista, pari kertaa kun putoaminen oli kuumottavan lahella. Nayapulista lahdin talsimaan kohti Birethania, laheista isompaa kylaa minne meni jopa tie. Taksikuski ois kovasti halunnut vieda meikalaisen sinne kun olin vahan hukassa kartalla, alkaen Rs300 hinnoilla ja viimeinen tarjous joka kuului korviin oli Rs20. Vihaan taksikuskeja nykyaan niiiiin paljon. Noh, joltain paikalliselta farmerilta paadyin kysymaan suuntaa, ja aija alko nauraan ja osoitti painvastaiseen suuntaan. Ihan hyva alku.. Mutta sitten paasinkin kartalle ja otin matkalla kiinni eri bussissa matkustaneen espanjalaisen aijan, ja taitettiin matkaa yhdessa loppupaiva Hileen kylaan, jossa yovyttiin.

27.2<br>
And the trek started from my Pokhara guesthouse, with a rather long walk to the local bus station. I was geared up with 2 shoulder bags, that basically had the minimal equipment. One set of change clothes, a warm fleece and a light jacket. Some water, money, map etc. I managed to find the bus station finally, and after some searching also the 'hole' that sold tickets to busses. The busride itself was the most crazy in my life. For the first 10km I sat inside in an incredibly crowded bus, that broke down, of course. After this we changed to a second bus and I ended up enjoying the rooftop views. It was OK when going uphill, on a 500m high cliff but after reaching the top the driver switched to neutral and a guy motioned me to 'hold fast'. I was like <i>oh shit</i> as the bus began rumbling down with a speed that would make Einstein turn in his grave. I was sitting on top of a small rail holding it with my both hands, my feet on the slippery round edge of the bus roof. At every bounce my feet wanted to slip into the void below and in every outward turn I could enjoy the views straight below as the bus leaned over the chasm. Aiyeeh, a couple of times were near to take me to the shortcut down, but I managed to hold. <br>
Finally at Nayapul, I started walking. A taxi driver wanted to offer me a ride to Birethani, the first town after Nayapul, for Rs300. I just told him that I want to walk (duh??) and the final offer I hear him shouting at me was like Rs20. I hate taxi drivers. Of course I could not locate myself on the map properly and I ended up asking an old local farmer man for directions. He laughed at me and pointed into the opposite direction.. where I came from. A good start, I'm a fine trekker indeed.. On the way I catched up with a spanish man who had used a bus mine overtook in a blind corner, and we continued for the rest of the day together (he had a guide/porter). The first day ended at reaching the town of Hile, one town short of my original plan, but it was good enough.

Kakkospaiva buuttas kayntiin mukavasti ennen auringonnousua, kuitenkin valoisaan aikaan. Majapaikka oli laakson itarinteella, eli kesti useamman tunnin ennenkuin auringonvalo saapui hiostamaan. Lahdimme kuitenkin samalla hetkella kiipeamaan ylos lansirinnetta kivirappusia pitkin. Naita kivirappusia taalla on melkein jokapuolella missa on vahan enemman makea ja jota kayttavat myos muulit ja vesipuhvelit. Nostan hattua niille jotka ovat nama tehneet. Rappuset on tehty luonnollisista laattakivista, joista useimmat lahemmas neliometrin kokoisia. Lienevat todella vanhoja alunperin, ehka jopa keskiajoilta asti. Tassa nyt kuitenkin noustiin aika pitka matka, (etelaiseen) Ban Thantin kylaan, jonne oli noin 3280 rappusta. Valilla nama rappuset nousee yli 60 asteen kulmassa, eli sita ottaa kymmenen askelmaa ja pitaa hetken tauon ettei sydan rajahda. Espanjalainen herra jai pitamaan majaa Ban Thantiin oppaansa suosituksesta (opashan ottaa paivapalkkaa ja saa provision kun majoittaa maksavaan paikkaan). Lyottaydyin kuitenkin yhteen japanilaisen kaverin nimelta Masanobu (helppo muistaa, Masa) kanssa kohti Gorapania, olihan paivaa viela rutkasti jaljella. Reitilla otettiin kisaa kolmen ruotsalaisen kanssa joilla oli mukana reissupeikko nimelta Bobby (? linkkia tulee kunhan loydan). Gorapani saavutettiin helposti, eika siina mitaan kummempaa.
</td>
<td>
The second day booted off nicely before sunrise. It was already light, but due to the high peaks and the location of the inn in the eastern slope of the valley kept us in a shade for quite some hours. After we got our first rays of sunlight we were already starting to climb up the western side of the valley along a stone staircase. Now these stone staircases exists everywhere, where the path is somewhat popular. Mainly I think it's there to make it easier for the mules and water buffalos to walk, and avoid crumbling of the trails. This specific climb had 3280 stair steps on it, and at times these stairs go up easily in a 60 degree angle, which means there's more catching breath time than actual climbing. Well, after a tiring climb we made it to the (southern) village of Ban Thanti, where the Spanish guy decided to stay for the night. After all, his guide recommended him to do so (naturally the guide is paid on day basis and he gets a commission from the guest houses). I laughed at the idea, as it was barely noon, and continued the trek with a Japanese guy named Masanobu. We had a good time walking towards Gorapani, the weather was cool with clouds now, and we had (I had) a small mental racing with a group of 3 swedish guys heading to the same direction. We arrived at Gorapani around uh, 4 o'clock and chose a guesthouse that Masanobu had heard to be excellent. It was the second highest guest house in the town, with some pretty neat views. One hour later the swedes arrived and came to the same guest house. We had a nice evening eating good food, and I retired early.

1.3-2.3<br>
Noustiin ylos ennen auringonnousua ja suunnattiin katseemme kohti Poon Hillia. Kyseessa on suht korkea kukkula joka on melkeinpa turistirysa. Kylmassa aamussa hajoilin pakkaseen kun taskulampun valossa kiipesimme ylos (tosin hikihan siina tuli pidemman paalle) n. 3,2km korkeuteen Gorapanista, joka sijaitsee jossain 2,8km paikkeilla. Nakymat oli kuitenkin huikeet, ei niita sanoin voi kuvailla. Kuvat tulee sit joskus.
Lopulta mentiin takas alas syomaan aamupala. Tassa vaiheessa jouduin muuttamaan suunnitelmiani. Gorapanista pohjoiseen paivan kavelymatkan jalkeen olisi alkanut TIE, joka on rakennettu 2007-2009 valilla ja yltaa koko matkan Jomsomiin asti. Koska en halunnut kavella tieta pitkin, paatin lahtea itaan ruotsalaisten kanssa. Masa jatkoi yksin pohjoiseen, tarkoituksenaan ottaa bussi Tatopanista takaisin Pokharaan. Me kiipesimme taas rinnetta ylos harjulle, joka oli korkeampi kuin Poon Hill, jossain 3,4km paikkeilla. Siina alkoi olemaan jo aika vasynyt vaikka paiva oli alussa. Nakymat oli taas huikeita, mutten ottanut kuvia koska kamera oli ilmoittanut aikaisemmin akun olevan lopussa (mika ei ollut asian laita, pakkanen teki kepposet). Sitten olikin edessa pitka ylosalasylosalas ja lopulta loputon alas varjossa. Paiva oli jokseenkin kylma, mutten jaksanut laittaa enempaa vaatetta paalle koska pelkasin etta puskis hiki. No, saavuttiin lopulta Ban Thantiin (eri Ban Thanti... naita samannimisia paikkoja riittaa) jossa, kun istuin alas ja tilasin kolan, iski kuume. Ilmeisesti oli vahan liian kylma. Paatin jaada tahan kolmen talon kylaan lepaamaan ja swedut ja heidan oppaansa jatkoivat matkaa. Seuraavana paivana kuume oli poissa mutta paatin levyttaa varmuuden vuoksi. Mukana oli onneksi kirjallisuutta jota lueskelin aikani kuluksi ja nautin loistavasta ruuasta. Paiva oli myoskin erittain kylma ja pilvinen joten pysyin lahinna peiton alla.

1.3-2.3<br>
We got up before sunrise (for real this time) around 5am and headed out for Poon Hill. We're talking about a relatively high "hill" here, at the height of 3,2km. I enjoyed the nice frost in the early morning, but even though I was freezing my ass in the beginning the long climb from Gorapani (2,8km) made me warm up nicely in the next 30 minutes. The views from up there were breathtaking and only pictures can bring justice to them. Or actually only seeing it yourself can bring it justice but whatever. After enjoying the views and doing a dance scene with Bobby the troll (!) we headed back down for breakfast. This is where I had to change my plans, as I heard that there's now a new road going from the next big town all the way to Jomsom. Trekking on a roadside with taxis and busses swooshing past you isn't really what I came for, so I decided to head to east with the swedes. Masa headed to the town, where he was to take a bus back to Pokhara and continue his trip. We climbed again up, this time the west side of the valley on a very high cliff (3,4km) with some superior views of the Annapurna range. After this the trail went downupdownupdown and finally a long descend down to a village called Ban Thanti (Nepalese are hardly creative at naming places..). When I sat down I felt a fever rise, as I had been quite cold while walking down (it got cloudy and chilly) and I decided to stay the night there. The swedes continued their way onwards.  So I slept the night, the next day was also cloudy and I decided to rest more just in case, even though the fever had gone. Luckily I had a book to read (a real crap one though). Good food and nice atmosphere in this village of 3 houses.. in the bottom of a chasm.

I spent the night in awesome views of a big valley in the town of Chile. Next morning I continued across the valley and climbed back up into great heights. I also ended up donating Rs100 to a local village school for some strange reasons, and continued to the town of Jhinu. I think I look too much of a hippie right now. I laughed at them puffing uphill, imagining them on the steeper ascends nearer to Annapurna. They had quite some guides in the front and in the bag and I just grinned at the thought that it looked like the guides were shepherds.. And at the behind are the mandatory porters of every civilized western group, carrying backpacks and sacks of 50kg loads (really .. couldn't lift those myself. tried.) that hold the ladies makeup etc. Jeez..
